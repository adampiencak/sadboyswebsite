Yung Lean

Jonatan Aron Leandoer Håstad (born 18 July 1996), known professionally as Yung Lean, is a Swedish rapper, singer and songwriter. He is known for his unique and influential style and his prominence as one of the early pioneers of cloud rap. He is a member of the Sad Boys rap collective, whose members have provided production for much of his discography

‍

Early Life

Jonatan Aron Leandoer Håstad was born on July 18th, 1996, to Kristoffer Leandoer, a poet and literary translator, and Elsa Håstad, a human rights activist and diplomat. He spent parts of his early childhood in Minsk, Belarus, and later grew up in Södermalm, Stockholm. He developed an interest in graffiti in his early teenage years.

‍

Career

Yung Lean began making music with Yung Sherman and Gud in 2012. He would share his music on Soundcloud and Tumblr and performed his first show in Gothenburg in May 2013. Later in the year, his song Ginseng Strip 2002 went viral across social media, leading him to abrupt early success. In July, he released his debut mixtape, Unknown Death 2002.

In 2014, he along with the rest of Sad Boys embarked on the White Marble Tour, consisting of 24 shows across Europe, and later the Black Marble tour across North America. His performance at New York City's Webster Hall was received favourably in publications such as XXL and The New York Times.

In September of that year, he released Unknown Memory, his first studio album. It consisted of 13 tracks, and featured contributions from Travis Scott alongside production from Whitearmor and fellow Sad Boys members Yung Sherman and Gud (credited as Yung Gud).

By 2015, Leandoer had moved to Miami Beach, US to record his second studio album Warlord. During this time, he became addicted to drugs and suffered a psychosis, leading him to be hospitalised.

In February 2016, Warlord was released.

In May 2020, Yung Lean released Starz. It featured production from Whitearmor and vocal contributions from the likes of Ariel Pink and Playboi Carti. However, Playboi Carti's featured verse only appears on physical releases.
